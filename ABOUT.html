Types Of Watchs

The date of birth of a wristwatch is considered to be 1812. It was then that the clock was first worn on the wrist, and not in the pocket. Since then, the mechanism of watches has undergone significant changes, and today we have a variety of their variations. We will tell you about the main types of watches, the characteristics of which directly affect the accuracy of the course, as well as the convenience of operation.

So, according to the mechanism of operation, all wristwatch is divided into mechanical, quartz and electronic. Further among them, too, there are “subtypes”-mechanical with auto-back, kinetic, on a solar battery, as well as chronographs and watches-chronometers. All these are very interesting models, and they deserve to learn more about them.

Mechanical watches

Mechanical watches work on a spring that needs to be started from time to time. In this case, the spring will first be twisted, and then slowly spin and set in motion, which will move minute and time arrows through the same time intervals with the help of gears.

The accuracy of the course of such watches and their service life depends on the materials from which gears are produced, as well as crystals (stones) on which they are based. In elite watches, even precious stones can be used. Mechanical clocks work without additional power sources, it is enough only to regularly start them on your own.
Mechanical watches do not lose their popularity. This option is simple and familiar: he went into the category of solid classics, which no one thinks to abandon.

Quartz Watch on a sunny battery

Another variety of popular hours popular today is models operating on a sunny battery. Such quartz watches are very practical: they do not need an anchor or frequent replacement of batteries. They use energy from sunlight. Moreover, the light from a natural or artificial source, which is hit during the day on the clock, is enough for them to provide the mechanism with a long and stable operation.

Most models are enough only 10 minutes a day to “fade” the usual room light, and this energy is enough for them for a day. The battery does not need manual activation. And some modern watches of this group can work without food from light even up to two years in complete darkness. A battery in a watch on a sunny battery needs to be changed only once every 10-15 years.

What are the watches made of?

For the first time, wristwatch appeared in 1812: it was then that they replaced the pocket watch. Since then, the principle of operation of the mechanism and part of the wrist watches has practically not changed: the energy of the groovy spring is transmitted to the pendulum-balance, it fluctuates, the clock goes. Only the technologies of the manufacture of elements, materials, designs are changing, become more sophisticated and more precisely, but modern mechanical watches are arranged according to the same scheme. With the help of a clumsy head, it is necessary to twist the spring in the drum; She, spinning, will twist the drum itself; The drum will convey rotation to the gears of the gears, and from it the pulse will receive a trigger - it converts the rotational movement of the wheels into a vibrational movement of the balance. It is the frequency of fluctuations in the balance that determines the accuracy of the watch.

In mechanisms with a manual plant, the spring must be twisted manually. In mechanisms with the auto -back, this function is taken by the rotor, which rotates from the fact that the clock is on the hand and moves with it during the day, and transfers the energy of its rotation to the spring. In quartz mechanisms, a battery serves as a source of energy: it nourishes a quartz generator. Due to the fact that in the quartz mechanism there are fewer moving parts, it is much more reliable.
